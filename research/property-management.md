# Industry research: property management

Segment researched: **SMB-to-mid-market professional property management** — third-party property management companies and professional landlords managing roughly 50–5,000 units, US-centric English-speaking market. Researched 2026-07-16.

## Top commercial players

1. **AppFolio** — The category leader: unified "one login" platform with the industry's deepest agentic AI (Realm-X). Founded 2006. ~9.1M units under management (Q3 2025 SEC filing). Segment: upper-SMB to mid-market (50-unit minimum, optimized 200+). Per-unit pricing: Core ~$1.40/unit ($280/mo min) → Max ~$5/unit. Sources: appfolio.com/pricing, appfolio.com/features, appfolio.com/newsroom, sec.gov (APPF 10-Q Q3 2025), leasense.com Buildium-vs-AppFolio 2026, costbench.com.
2. **Buildium** — Accounting-first platform for small third-party PM companies and associations; RealPage-owned since Dec 2019 ($580M, 17k customers / ~2M units at close). Founded 2004. ~15–20k customers today (third-party estimates conflict; treat as range). Segment: SMB (sweet spot ~50–500 units). Flat tiers: Essential $62/mo, Growth $192/mo, Premium $400/mo + heavy per-transaction fees. Sources: buildium.com/features, buildium.com/pricing, RealPage acquisition PR (sec.gov), G2/Capterra review summaries.
3. **DoorLoop** — Fastest-rising challenger: Capterra Shortlist #1 two years running (96/100), $130M raised (JMI Series B). Founded 2019. ~10,000+ customers in 100+ countries. Segment: SMB (sweet spot 20–200 units; 96.5% of G2 reviewers are small-business). Tiers: Starter $69/mo (annual), Pro $149, Premium $209. Sources: doorloop.com/features, doorloop.com/pricing, doorloop.com/ai-assistant, capterra.com shortlist 2026, refreshmiami.com funding coverage.
4. **Yardi Breeze** — The enterprise incumbent's SMB play: "refreshingly simple" self-service edition running on Voyager's accounting engine; true multi-asset-class (residential, commercial, affordable, HOA). Launched 2018 (parent Yardi Systems, 1984). Crossed 1M units on Breeze/Breeze Premier (July 2025 Yardi PR). Segment: SMB to lower mid-market (≤1,000-unit design center). $1/unit/mo ($100 min); Premier $400/mo min. Sources: yardibreeze.com/pricing, yardibreeze.com/features, yardi.com press releases, mochamanage.com review.
5. **Rentvine** — Modern trust-accounting-first platform for professional (especially single-family) PM companies; the frequently cited switch-away destination from AppFolio/Propertyware. Founded ~2016 (Estero, FL). No public customer counts; $74M growth capital (Mainsail Partners, Aug 2024). Segment: SMB-to-mid-market third-party PMs. Single all-inclusive SKU ~$1.50–2.50/unit, $199/mo min (quote-only). Sources: rentvine.com/features, rentvine.com/pricing, rentvine.com/ai-assistant, help.rentvine.com, mainsailpartners.com, softwareadvice.com profile.

Market-shape notes (context, not matrix columns): the market is three-tiered — enterprise suites (Yardi Voyager, RealPage, MRI, Entrata; out of scope), the commercial SMB/mid-market tier above, and a DIY-landlord freemium tail (TenantCloud, TurboTenant, Innago, RentRedi, Avail, Hemlane; 1–20 units, <$30/mo economics — researched and deliberately excluded from the matrix for segment coherence). **Rent Manager** (LCS, founded 1987, 10k+ companies, Capterra #3) is a credible sixth player omitted only because its capabilities were not verified page-by-page in this pass. Consolidation is partial: RealPage runs Buildium + Propertyware as separate brands; portals bought the DIY tools (Realtor.com→Avail). Growth capital is flowing to independents (DoorLoop $130M, Rentvine $74M). AI is the active feature battleground, landing in leasing and maintenance first; third-party trackers put PM AI adoption at ~21% (2024) → ~34% (2025) (usehaven.ai).

## Synthesized capabilities

### Must-have (v1)

Present in at least 4 of 5 players — table-stakes for the segment:

- **Property & unit portfolio management** — properties, units, tenancies, and ownership entities as the core data model.
- **Lease lifecycle management & e-signature** — templates, auto-populated lease data, execution, renewals.
- **Online rent collection** — ACH/card autopay with late-fee automation, via licensed payment-partner rails (never in-house money movement).
- **Resident & owner portals** — portal-per-persona is universal across all five players.
- **Maintenance requests & work orders** — resident intake with photos, triage, vendor assignment, status tracking.
- **Vendor management & payments** — vendor records, work-order assignment, bill entry, outbound payments.
- **Tenant screening** — credit/criminal/eviction via an FCRA-compliant consumer-reporting-agency partner.
- **Online rental applications & applicant tracking** — application forms, applicant-paid fees, lead-to-lease pipeline.
- **Vacancy listing & syndication** — publish vacancies to the major listing networks (Zillow group et al.).
- **Double-entry property + trust accounting** — GL, AP/AR, bank reconciliation, and manager-vs-client fund separation with audit trail.
- **Owner statements & reporting** — automated periodic statements, distributions, and financial reports per owner/property.
- **1099 e-filing** — owner and vendor 1099-MISC/NEC generation and IRS e-file.
- **Communication hub** — templated email/SMS, bulk messaging, threaded history tied to entities.
- **Document storage & templates** — files attached to properties/leases/tenants with template support.

### Differentiator (v1)

Deep in only 1–2 players, high impact — the "why us" for an AI-native platform built on Plenipo:

- **Agentic AI across leasing, maintenance, and accounting** — 24/7 lead auto-response and showing scheduling, conversational maintenance intake and triage, invoice→GL extraction. Only AppFolio (Realm-X) ships this end-to-end today, and competitors gate it to top tiers; a Plenipo build gets it natively from day one, un-gated.
- **Compliance-grade trust accounting with full audit trail** — automatic manager/client fund separation aimed at state broker trust-account rules. Rentvine exemplifies it and wins switchers on it; verified-deep in only that one player.
- **Open API + MCP server for external AI** — programmatic and LLM access to platform data under RBAC. Rentvine's beta MCP server is the only one in market; AppFolio gates its API to the Max tier. Plenipo's agent/MCP infrastructure makes this nearly free to ship.

### Skip for v1

Adversarially checked: three of these are present in 4–5 players by count, and are skipped anyway with the reason stated — the count rule alone doesn't survive contact with v1 economics.

- **Renters insurance program** (present in all 5) — requires licensed insurance sellers/partnerships; not a software-only capability. Revisit post-v1 as ancillary revenue.
- **Website builder for the PM company** (present in all 5) — a separate product line; target customers already have websites. Integration/embed path later.
- **Inspections** (present in 4) — two of the four gate it behind add-ons, and Rentvine ships without it via integrations (zInspector/RentCheck), proving the market accepts an integration path. Strong post-v1 AI candidate (photo analysis), so defer, don't drop.
- **Maintenance contact center / 24-7 human coordination** — a staffed service business, not software; the AI maintenance-intake differentiator covers the same job.
- **Utility billing / convergent billing** — deep only in AppFolio; metering/RUBS is a niche adjacent business.
- **Community-association (HOA/condo) management** — a distinct vertical (boards, violations, dues) deep only in Buildium.
- **Commercial property support (CAM reconciliation)** — a distinct vertical deep in DoorLoop/Breeze; v1 is long-term residential only.
- **Tenant benefits / ancillary revenue packages** — partner-dependent monetization, weak presence across players.
- **Affordable housing / Section 8 compliance** — heavy specialized compliance (HUD/LIHTC) present only as enterprise-tier add-ons.
- **DIY-landlord freemium tier** — different buyer, different economics (freemium + transaction-fee monetization); do not chase TenantCloud/TurboTenant down-market.

## Notable UX patterns observed

- **Portal-per-persona** — separate resident, owner, vendor, and applicant portals hung off one operator console. Seen in: all five (Rentvine and TenantCloud even ship four persona apps).
- **The accounting ledger is the product's spine** — live ledgers on every entity organize the whole UX; accounting quality is the #1 cited strength/weakness in reviews. Seen in: Buildium, Rentvine, Yardi Breeze (Voyager engine), AppFolio.
- **Single-login all-in-one consolidation** — marketed as replacing many point tools ("consolidated nine systems into one"); central dashboard with function-based navigation (Leasing / Accounting / Operations). Seen in: AppFolio, DoorLoop, Rentvine.
- **AI embedded inside workflows, not a bolt-on chatbot** — assistant lives in the app ("no new logins"), acts on live data, drafts within the workflow it serves. Seen in: AppFolio (Realm-X), DoorLoop (AI Assistant), Rentvine ("AI lives inside every workflow").
- **Tier-gating as monetization** — per-use fees (eSign, EFT, screening) that cheapen as the tier rises; API/CRM/AI gated to top tiers. Seen in: Buildium, AppFolio, DoorLoop, Yardi Breeze. (Rentvine counter-positions with a single all-inclusive SKU.)
- **Self-onboarding simplicity as the wedge** — guided setup, free migration/training, "refreshingly simple" positioning wins the SMB tier per review sentiment. Seen in: Yardi Breeze, DoorLoop, Buildium.

## Compliance / regulatory considerations

- **State trust-accounting rules (real-estate commissions)** — client funds (rent, deposits) must be segregated from the manager's operating funds, with full audit trails and reconciliation (commonly three-way: bank / ledger / property). Constrains the ledger design: fund-separated double-entry books, immutable audit log, per-state configuration. This is the compliance capability PM companies switch vendors over (Rentvine's wedge).
- **FCRA (Fair Credit Reporting Act)** — tenant screening must run through a compliant consumer reporting agency with permissible purpose, applicant consent, and adverse-action notices. Constrains architecture: screening as a partner integration (TransUnion et al.), stored adverse-action records, strict access control on reports.
- **Fair Housing Act (+ HUD guidance on AI/algorithmic screening)** — no discriminatory criteria in advertising, screening, or AI-driven lead handling; disparate-impact risk applies to models. Constrains AI design: log agent decisions and criteria, keep humans in the loop on adverse outcomes, no protected-class proxies in prompts/models.
- **IRS 1099-MISC/NEC reporting** — annual e-filing for owner and vendor payments above the reporting threshold ($600 for 2025 filings); IRIS/FIRE e-file formats. Constrains the accounting data model (payee TINs, payment categorization) and demands an e-file workflow.
- **Money-transmission / payments compliance** — moving rent between tenants, trust accounts, and owners triggers money-transmitter licensing if done in-house. Constraint: all money movement through a licensed processor partner (NACHA rules for ACH, PCI-DSS scope pushed to the processor).
- **State security-deposit statutes** — per-state caps, interest accrual, and return deadlines. Constrains deposit handling: state-configurable rules engine, deadline tracking.
- **Data privacy (CCPA/state equivalents) + SOC 2** — tenant PII (SSNs from applications, financial data) at rest; SaaS-baseline expectations of encryption, RBAC, audit logging, breach process. Aligns with Plenipo's existing tenant-isolation and RBAC model.

## Open questions for the user

All decisions were delegated to the agent for this run ("you take all the decisions"); answers are recorded in-place below.

1. **Which customer segment does the product target — third-party PM companies, or DIY landlords?**
   **Answer (delegated decision):** Third-party property management companies and professional landlords in the ~50–2,000 door range (the Buildium/Rentvine segment). This is where trust accounting, RBAC, and multi-tenancy matter — Plenipo's strengths — and it avoids the freemium DIY tier's monetization trap.
2. **Geography for v1?**
   **Answer (delegated decision):** US-only. The compliance spine (trust accounting, FCRA, 1099, security-deposit statutes) is US-specific; internationalization is a post-v1 concern.
3. **How is money movement handled?**
   **Answer (delegated decision):** Exclusively through a licensed payment-processor partner behind a provider-agnostic abstraction; never in-house money transmission. Concrete processor choice belongs to the architecture phase.
4. **Which property verticals are in scope for v1?**
   **Answer (delegated decision):** Long-term residential rentals only. No commercial/CAM, no HOA/associations, no short-term/vacation, no affordable-housing compliance (all in Skip for v1).
5. **Which screening partner?**
   **Answer (delegated decision):** Deferred to the architecture phase. The spec should assume an FCRA-compliant CRA integration behind an abstraction (TransUnion-style API), with adverse-action record storage as a first-class requirement.
6. **Four matrix cells are best-guess (footnotes ¹–⁴) — is that acceptable to proceed on?**
   **Answer (delegated decision):** Yes. None of the guessed cells changes the must-have / differentiator / skip classification: trust accounting is must-have regardless of AppFolio/Buildium's exact depth, and the task-management/tenant-benefits guesses sit in buckets unaffected by one signal level.
